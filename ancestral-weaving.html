The Ancestral Weaving

A living archive of blood lineage — each ancestor grounded in their terroir, their unresolved patterns witnessed with truth and innerstanding, their threads woven forward into the present.

This is not a single thread. These ancestors arrive from many separate branches of the lineage — Celtic, Norse, Norman, Breton, Gaelic, Scottish, Welsh, American — each from their own direction, through their own century, along their own route. They do not all connect to each other. They connect to the same living descendant from entirely different directions, converging in one body the way rivers converge at a delta. The weaving holds the convergence. The genealogical chart holds the individual lines. These are not the same document.

The Druidic triquetra on the left shoulder — land, sea, sky; past, present, future; maiden, mother, crone — is carried in the body as a living sigil of this weaving.
